Case Study

Customer Issue Reporting Dashboard

A support operations reporting model for tracking repeated customer issues, resolution status, priority, affected workflows, and patterns that can help teams improve service quality.

Support Scenario

A support team receives repeated issues across accounts, bookings, access, integrations, or platform workflows. Without reporting, repeated problems remain hidden and escalations lack business context.

Tools Represented

  • Google Sheets for tracking and filtering
  • CRM exports for customer and ticket context
  • GA4 or GTM when web behavior needs review
  • Support tags for category and priority analysis

Dashboard Fields

What I would track.

Field Purpose
Ticket ID Links the report back to the original support case.
Issue Category Groups issues by account, access, booking, billing, integration, or platform.
Priority Shows urgency and customer impact.
Status Tracks open, pending, escalated, resolved, or follow-up needed.
Root Cause Documents the confirmed or suspected cause once investigated.
Resolution Notes Captures what was done and what the customer was told.

Result

What this proves.

This case study shows my operations mindset: I can turn support activity into organized data, identify repeated issue patterns, improve escalation quality, and support better service decisions.
